## How this is organised

Three tiers, by how the v30 manuscript should use each template:

- **Tier 1 — Structural mirror.** The paper whose section structure, table set, and flow the v30 manuscript should emulate end-to-end. One paper.
- **Tier 2 — Argumentation-style mirror.** Papers whose voice, hedging vocabulary, claim-ceiling phrasing, and reviewer-anticipation patterns the v30 manuscript should adopt. Three papers.

Each entry carries: (a) why it's an exemplar, (b) what's already in v30, (c) what argumentation-style features the v30 should still emulate, (d) specific risks.

## Tier 1 — Structural mirror

### T1. Aguinis, Ramani & Alabduljader (2023), *Organizational Research Methods*

**Citation.** Aguinis, H., Ramani, R. S., & Alabduljader, N. (2023). Best-practice recommendations for producers, evaluators, and users of methodological literature reviews. *Organizational Research Methods*, 26(1), 46–76. DOI: [10.1177/1094428120943281](https://doi.org/10.1177/1094428120943281). Local PDF: [`template_pdfs/aguinis_ramani_alabduljader_2023_ORM_methodological_reviews_v1.pdf`](template_pdfs/aguinis_ramani_alabduljader_2023_ORM_methodological_reviews_v1.pdf).

**Why still the closest single template (still applicable for v30).** Same journal. Same genre (categorise + content-analyse + recommend). Comparable evidentiary scale (168 reviews × 7 categories vs. 36 cases × 17 roles × 6 statuses). Same three-part contribution shape. Same tripartite stakeholder framing.

**What's already in v30 (per `transplant_specs.md` Transplant 1 + iterations P–CC).**
- Tripartite "This paper makes three contributions" structure in abstract — preserved through v25 phenomenon-first rewrite.
- §3.0 Exemplars subsection — present, naming one case per status.
- Table 5 (stakeholder × stage) and Table 6 (status × imperative + coordination mechanism) — present, named for the three stakeholder groups (methods researchers, reviewers/editors, institutional actors).
- Released artifact paired with the framework (the 612-cell coding CSV, codebook, decision tree, reviewer-audit checklist).

**Argumentation-style features the v30 should emulate but does not yet.**

1. **Direct first-person institutional voice.** Aguinis 2023 abstract: *"We categorized and content-analyzed 168 methodological literature reviews… First, our categorization uncovered… Second, our content analysis uncovered… Third, we describe choices and judgment calls."* The voice is **active, confident, declarative**. v30's abstract starts with the central finding (good — phenomenon-first per Mintzberg) but then reverts to passive constructions ("Three contributions follow…" vs. Aguinis's "*We* categorized"). v30 lines 5: replace "Three contributions follow" with "We make three contributions" to land the active register.

2. **Confident contribution-claim verbs.** Aguinis 2023 uses "uncovered," "created," "describe," "offers" — *transitive* and *active*. v30 uses "recasts," "develops," "applies" — also good — but then immediately hedges: "the paper's claim ceiling is set at comparative typology audit, not validated theory of automated science." Aguinis 2023 *never apologises in the abstract*. The hedge that v30 carries is appropriate given the κ failure, but the *vocabulary* of the hedge could be tighter: "deliberately bounded at the audit-with-framework grain" rather than the current double-disclaimer ("set at … not at …").

3. **Stakeholder-specific normative voice in the conclusion.** Aguinis 2023 conclusion (pp. 68–70) directly addresses producers, evaluators, users with imperative recommendations. v30's Tables 5–6 do the structural work, but the *prose* preceding the tables is now compressed (v25 trim) to one sentence ("Tables 5 and 6 translate the descriptive audit into recommendations…"). The Aguinis 2023 register would expand this to one paragraph per stakeholder, restating in prose what each gets from the framework.

**What the v30 manuscript still has that the template would tighten.**
- **κ disclosure timing.** Aguinis 2023 reports inter-coder agreement in §Method (single mention). v30 reports κ = 0.289 in the abstract and again in §6 with three priority-ordered readings. The Aguinis 2023 placement — single, methods-section — is a stronger position because the κ is not allowed to colonise the substantive findings. v30 could move the κ disclosure entirely into §6 and the Transparency block, with one cross-reference from the abstract ("inter-coder reliability is reported in §6 and §Transparency").

**Risks of over-emulating.**
- Aguinis 2023 has 168 published reviews as its evidentiary base — peer-reviewed, complete, homogeneous. v30 has 36 cases mixing peer-reviewed + preprint + autonomy-heavy boundary systems. The more confident voice that fits Aguinis 2023's evidentiary footing would over-claim if applied to v30's heterogeneous corpus. **Borrow the structure and the active verbs; keep v30's scope-condition discipline.**

## Tier 2 — Argumentation-style mirrors

### T2. Aguinis & Solarino (2019), *Strategic Management Journal*

**Citation.** Aguinis, H., & Solarino, A. M. (2019). Transparency and replicability in qualitative research: The case of interviews with elite informants. *Strategic Management Journal*, 40(8), 1291–1315. DOI: [10.1002/smj.3015](https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.3015). Local PDF: [`template_pdfs/aguinis_solarino_2019_SMJ_transparency_replicability_elite_interviews.pdf`](template_pdfs/aguinis_solarino_2019_SMJ_transparency_replicability_elite_interviews.pdf).

**Why argumentation-style mirror.** This paper does what v30 is doing: audits a comparable-N corpus (52 articles), reports a reliability concern in main text, and offers a behaviorally-anchored framework as the deliverable. Its argumentation style is the strongest match for v30's evidentiary footing.

**Argumentation-style features the v30 should emulate.**

1. **Explicit ontological declaration in §1.** Aguinis & Solarino p. 1292: *"Before describing our study, we clarify two important issues: Our ontological perspective and the desirability of replicability in qualitative research. First, our ontological and epistemological perspective is 'qualitative positivism' as used by Eisenhardt (1989)… This ontological perspective is dominant in strategy."* This is exactly the move v30 needs and almost has — v30 §2 declares "modernist measurement tradition" and lists adjacent literatures as scope conditions, but does so in a single dense paragraph at line 49. **Borrow the move-to-§1 placement and the "Before describing our study, we clarify…" rhetorical opening.** That single sentence converts the scope conditions from defensive disclaimer to confident declaration.

2. **Honest discussion of contestation.** Aguinis & Solarino p. 1292: *"regarding the desirability of replicability, in contrast to quantitative research, this is a potentially contentious issue in qualitative research. For example, in ethnography replicability is not necessarily meaningful…"* The paper *names* its contested terrain rather than retreating from it. v30's §2 paradigm-declaration paragraph names many contested terrains (paradox, institutional theory, practice theory, evolutionary theory) but does so in a single block; the Solarino move would distribute these across §§1, 2, 6 so each contested terrain is named where the paper makes the move that contests it.

3. **Catalyst voice in conclusion.** Aguinis & Solarino abstract closer: *"We hope our article will serve as a catalyst for improving the degree of transparency and replicability of future qualitative research."* This is hedged-but-confident: "we hope," "serve as a catalyst," "improving" — a pattern of *forward-looking generative claim without prescriptive over-reach*. v30's §7 closing line ("not from human research to machine research, but from one research role system to another") is rhetorically stronger but **does not name what the framework is for**. Replace or supplement with a Solarino-style "we hope this rubric serves as a catalyst for…" sentence to link the abstract claim to the conclusion close.

**Risks.** SMJ register is more punchy than ORM. The "managerial summary" structure is SMJ-specific and should not be transplanted.

### T3. Hickman, Thapa, Tay, Cao & Srinivasan (2022), *Organizational Research Methods*

**Citation.** Hickman, L., Thapa, S., Tay, L., Cao, M., & Srinivasan, P. (2022). Text preprocessing for text mining in organizational research: Review and recommendations. *Organizational Research Methods*, 25(1), 114–146. DOI: [10.1177/1094428120971683](https://doi.org/10.1177/1094428120971683). Local PDF: [`template_pdfs/hickman_thapa_tay_cao_srinivasan_2022_ORM_text_preprocessing.pdf`](template_pdfs/hickman_thapa_tay_cao_srinivasan_2022_ORM_text_preprocessing.pdf).

**Why argumentation-style mirror.** Hickman 2022 is the canonical ORM specimen of *moderator-conditional recommendations* — the voice ORM expects when a methods paper makes prescriptive claims under uncertainty.

**Argumentation-style features the v30 should emulate.**

1. **Moderator-conditional recommendation register.** Hickman 2022 abstract: *"We conduct two complementary reviews of computational linguistics and organizational text mining research to provide empirically grounded text preprocessing decision-making recommendations that account for the type of text mining conducted (i.e., open or closed vocabulary), the research question under investigation, and the data set's characteristics… Notably, deviations from these recommendations will be appropriate and, at times, necessary due to the unique characteristics of one's text data."* This is the **conditional-confident** voice: declarative claims that explicitly carry their moderators on their face. v30 has the moderator-conditional structure (Table 6 status × imperative × coordination mechanism) but the prose around the table does not match the Hickman register. **Borrow the "*notably, deviations from these recommendations will be appropriate*" closing structure** for the introductory paragraph to Tables 5–6 — it lands the prescriptive claims while pre-empting reviewer over-application.

2. **Two-lens framing as a cumulative-knowledge move.** Hickman 2022 frames the two reviews as triangulation. v30's two-lens framing (case family vs. AI capability) is now a single sentence at §6 line 195. Hickman 2022's voice would expand this to a paragraph — naming each lens, its evidence base, and the verdict it licenses, with a one-sentence "convergence" close.

3. **Explicit decision-factor presentation.** Hickman 2022 has Table 6 (decision-factor × recommendation grid). v30 has Tables 5 (stakeholder × stage) and 6 (status × imperative × coordination mechanism). The Hickman 2022 *voice* introducing those tables is what v30 is missing — a paragraph that names *the decision factors* the table is conditioning on (in v30: the role's status, the case-family context, the AI-capability label) before presenting the recommendations.

**Risks.** Hickman 2022 is *more* methods-advice and *less* substantive-finding than v30 wants to be. Take the conditional-confident voice; do not let it pull the v30 prose toward methods-advice when the v30 thesis (epistemic-guardian configuration) is substantive.

### T4. Pratt, Kaplan & Whittington (2020), *Administrative Science Quarterly*

**Citation.** Pratt, M. G., Kaplan, S., & Whittington, R. (2020). Editorial essay: The tumult over transparency: Decoupling transparency from replication in establishing trustworthy qualitative research. *Administrative Science Quarterly*, 65(1), 1–19.

**Why argumentation-style mirror (NEW — not in v11-era list).** This editorial essay is the reigning ORM-adjacent statement on how a qualitative-coded methods paper should *position* itself relative to replication-tradition expectations. v30 cites it in §6 as the κ-failure framing literature (BG079); but the v30 should emulate its **rhetorical structure for handling the trustworthiness-vs-replication contestation**, not just cite it.

**Argumentation-style features the v30 should emulate.**

1. **Decoupling-as-positioning move.** Pratt-Kaplan-Whittington's central rhetorical move: *decouple* transparency from replication so that a paper can claim transparency strongly without claiming replicability. v30 §6 cites this move but applies it weakly ("we follow Pratt, Kaplan & Whittington (2020) in treating *transparency* and *replication* as decoupleable goals"). The Pratt et al. *voice* would convert this from a citation to a load-bearing claim: "We claim transparency without claiming full replication: the rubric, the released coding CSV, and the v0.1–v0.4 codebook history are the transparency commitments; the κ ≥ 0.60 threshold the rubric did not meet is a replication commitment we declare unmet."

2. **Editorial-essay register.** Pratt et al. write in first-person plural with strong direct claims and explicit position-taking ("we argue," "we propose," "we contend"). v30 uses direct claims but often softens them with parentheticals. **Strip the "in our view" / "we suggest" type parentheticals from §§1, 6, 7** for a more authoritative voice.

**Risks.** This is an editorial essay, not a research article — its voice is more polemical than ORM allows for an empirical-audit submission. Borrow the decoupling move and the editorial-essay confidence; do not borrow the polemical extension.

### T6. Tay, Woo, Hickman, Booth & D'Mello (2022), *Advances in Methods and Practices in Psychological Science*

**Citation.** Tay, L., Woo, S. E., Hickman, L., Booth, B. M., & D'Mello, S. (2022). A conceptual framework for investigating and mitigating machine-learning measurement bias (MLMB) in psychological assessment. *AMPPS*, 5(1). Local PDF in `template_pdfs/`.

**Specific transplantable move.** **Workflow-figure paired with diagnostic-criteria table.** v30 has Figure 1 (coder-workflow schematic) and Table 1 (rubric); they are not yet *paired* in the Tay 2022 sense. Tay 2022 Figure 1 is a pipeline; Figure 4 is an expanded Brunswik lens. v30's Figure 1 should be reframed as the *applied-use* pipeline (a coder receives a case → applies the decision tree → assigns a status), not as the rubric-development workflow. Argumentation-style note: Tay 2022's voice is highly conceptual and *low on empirical evidence*; v30 is the opposite. The figure-text pairing transfers; the conceptual register does not.

### T7. Ziems, Held, Shaikh, Chen, Zhang & Yang (2024), *Computational Linguistics*

**Citation.** Ziems, C., Held, W., Shaikh, O., Chen, J., Zhang, Z., & Yang, D. (2024). Can large language models transform computational social science? *Computational Linguistics*, 50(1), 237–291. arXiv: [2305.03514](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.03514).

**Specific transplantable move.** **Crisp numbered conclusion: "Augment the pipeline in two ways."** v30's §7 closing already has P1–P3 (forward propositions) but the *voice* of the close is more reflective than crisp. Ziems et al. 2024's closing register would tighten v30's §7 close to: "(i) the rubric augments existing methods-journal review by adding a role-cell-level coding instrument; (ii) the released artifacts (CSV, codebook, decision tree, reviewer-audit checklist) augment the methodological-literature workflow with an applied-use pipeline; (iii) the 2027 hold-out augments the disconfirmation apparatus." Argumentation-style note: ACL register is too benchmark-flavoured for ORM; take the *structure* (numbered close), not the *tone*.

### T8. Bergh, Sharp, Aguinis, Li & Boyd (2017), *Strategic Organization* (NEW — argumentation-style exemplar)

**Citation.** Bergh, D. D., Sharp, B. M., Aguinis, H., & Li, M. (2017). Is there a credibility crisis in strategic management research? Evidence on the reproducibility of study findings. *Strategic Organization*, 15(3), 423–436.

**Specific transplantable move.** **The credibility-crisis voice on a reliability-failure finding.** Bergh et al. 2017 reports that 70% of replication attempts failed to reproduce findings — a *headline negative result* about the field's methodology. They handle the failure by: (a) reporting it bluntly in the abstract, (b) framing it as a research-quality opportunity rather than a crisis-of-confidence, (c) offering specific-action prescriptions for authors, reviewers, and editors. v30's κ = 0.289 disclosure has the same shape (headline negative result) and could borrow the Bergh et al. handling: **bluntly stated, framed as opportunity, paired with specific prescriptions**. v30 already does (a) and partially (c); (b) is what's missing — the κ failure is currently framed as "a known limitation" with v1.1 remedies, not as "an opportunity for the field to develop calibration-session protocols for AI-embedded coding."

### T9. Edwards (2011), *Organizational Research Methods* (NEW — argumentation-style exemplar)

**Citation.** Edwards, J. R. (2011). The fallacy of formative measurement. *Organizational Research Methods*, 14(2), 370–388.

**Specific transplantable move.** **The polemical-but-cited register.** Edwards 2011 is a *polemical* paper (the title carries "fallacy"); it is rigorously argued and heavily cited in ORM. v30 cites it (BG090) as the formative-measurement reference. The *argumentation style* — naming a fallacy and then methodically dismantling it — is the closest ORM precedent for v30's "this is not a measurement-instrument paper, it is a typology-emergence audit under reliability constraint" positional move. v30 currently makes this move quietly (per §genre-level diagnosis in `template_vs_manuscript_differences.md`) but Edwards 2011's register would let it be made *explicitly*.

### T10. Vandenberg & Lance (2000), *Organizational Research Methods* (NEW — argumentation-style exemplar)

**Citation.** Vandenberg, R. J., & Lance, C. E. (2000). A review and synthesis of the measurement invariance literature: Suggestions, practices, and recommendations for organizational research. *Organizational Research Methods*, 3(1), 4–70.

**Specific transplantable move.** **The "review-and-synthesis-with-recommendations" subtitle structure.** This is the most cited ORM methods-rubric paper in the v30 reference list. Its title structure (*Review and Synthesis of X: Suggestions, Practices, and Recommendations*) is the canonical ORM methods-rubric title pattern and v30 should consider matching it: *"Comparative Typology Audit of Role-System Change Under AI Embedding: A Behaviorally-Anchored Rubric Applied to 36 Cases"* mirrors this structure. Argumentation-style note: v30 currently has *"From Repository to Role System: A Comparative Typology Audit of 36 Cases"* — a finding-led title rather than an instrument-led title. The Vandenberg & Lance pattern is instrument-led. **This is a positioning choice**: finding-led titles are stronger when the finding is the contribution; instrument-led titles are stronger when the rubric is the contribution. v30 has been reframed (via the X-iteration through CC-iteration loop) to position the rubric *as* the primary contribution with the substantive finding as evidence — under that positioning, an instrument-led title would be more accurate.

## Argumentation-style synthesis

Across the 10 templates, six recurrent argumentation patterns appear that the v30 manuscript should emulate:

### Pattern 1 — Active-voice contribution claims with declarative verbs

**Templates:** Aguinis 2023, Aguinis & Solarino 2019, Hickman 2022, Bergh et al. 2017.

**Vocabulary:** "We categorized," "we audited," "we developed," "we offer," "we propose," "we find."

**v30 currently uses:** "Three contributions follow" (passive); "the framework engages" (impersonal); "we acknowledge" (defensive).

**Suggested v30 rewrite:** Convert the abstract's "Three contributions follow" to "We make three contributions"; convert §2's "the framework engages some adjacent literatures more deeply than others" to "We engage some adjacent literatures more deeply than others"; convert §6's "we acknowledge a deeper limitation" to "We declare a deeper limitation."

### Pattern 2 — Conditional-confident hedging vocabulary

**Templates:** Hickman 2022, Tay 2022, Pratt-Kaplan-Whittington 2020.

**Vocabulary:** "Best practice," "candidate framework," "deviations will be appropriate," "decoupleable goals," "intended applied use."

**v30 currently uses:** "Preliminary framework" (was the v11 vocabulary; partially carried into v30 abstract); "comparative typology audit, not validated theory" (double-disclaimer).

**Suggested v30 rewrite:** Replace "preliminary framework" / "comparative typology audit, not validated theory" with **"candidate framework with a behaviorally-anchored rubric"** — single positive descriptor, no double-disclaimer.

### Pattern 3 — Stakeholder-named conclusion register

**Templates:** Aguinis 2023, Aguinis-Villamor 2021, Pratt-Kaplan-Whittington 2020.

**Structure:** "For [audience X], the framework offers [specific deliverable]." Repeated for 2–3 audience groups.

**v30 currently has:** Tables 5–6 do this structurally but the prose around the tables (§7 line 245) is one sentence. The full per-stakeholder paragraph that *transplant_specs.md* §1c specified for v12 was trimmed back during the X–CC iterations.

**Suggested v30 rewrite:** Restore one paragraph (≈100 words) of per-stakeholder prose immediately before Table 5, addressing methods researchers, reviewers/editors, and institutional actors in turn.

### Pattern 4 — Reliability disclosure as forward-looking opportunity

**Templates:** Bergh et al. 2017, Pratt-Kaplan-Whittington 2020, Aguinis & Solarino 2019.

**Move:** When a paper reports a reliability-failure finding (like v30's κ = 0.289), it frames the failure as an *opportunity* — for the field, for future research, for instrument development — rather than as a defect of this particular paper.

**v30 currently has:** κ failure framed as "a known limitation" with v1.1 instrument commitments. The instrument-development frame is partial; the *field-opportunity* frame is missing.

**Suggested v30 rewrite:** Add one sentence in §6 immediately after the κ disclosure: *"The structural failure on the *conditional* and *transformed* anchors is itself diagnostic of where the field needs new calibration-session protocols and longitudinal transition coding; we treat this rubric and its κ as the v1.0 baseline for future development."*

### Pattern 5 — Ontological / paradigm declaration in §1, not §2

**Templates:** Aguinis & Solarino 2019, Pratt-Kaplan-Whittington 2020.

**Move:** Declare ontological position early (§1), not as a §2 scope condition. Aguinis & Solarino: "Before describing our study, we clarify two important issues: Our ontological perspective and the desirability of replicability." The early declaration converts a defensive disclaimer into a confident positional move.

**v30 currently has:** Paradigm declaration in §2 line 49 (added in v29 BB-iteration). This is *too late* in the manuscript by Aguinis & Solarino standards.

**Suggested v30 rewrite:** Move the paradigm-declaration paragraph from §2 to a new §1 ¶3 (between current line 13 and line 15), opening with "Before describing the audit, we clarify the rubric's paradigm location." Length: 100–120 words. Compensating cut: trim the §2 version to one sentence cross-referencing §1.

### Pattern 6 — Crisp numbered close

**Templates:** Ziems et al. 2024, Aguinis-Villamor 2021.

**Move:** Conclusion ends with numbered list of takeaways or research directions, *not* with reflective prose.

**v30 currently has:** §7 closing is "The empirical terrain has changed: not from human research to machine research, but from one research role system to another" — rhetorical, not numbered.

**Suggested v30 rewrite:** Replace the final paragraph of §7 with a numbered three-point close: "(1) the rubric is offered as a v1.0 measurement instrument for role-status change under AI embedding; (2) the released artifacts (CSV, codebook, decision tree, reviewer-audit checklist) constitute the applied-use pipeline; (3) the 2027 hold-out and parallel-rubric application are the disconfirmation apparatus." Keep the "research role system" sentence as the penultimate, single-line rhetorical close after the numbered points.
