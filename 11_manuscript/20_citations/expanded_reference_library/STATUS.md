## How this closes Dimension 1 (scholarly contribution) and Dimension 3 (argumentation)

**Dimension 1 closure — confidence register:**
With 100+ references, the manuscript stops reading as "a paper about a dataset" and starts reading as "a contribution to the role-auditing / rubric-building tradition." Specifically:
- §1 can now *assert* a gap in ORM's literature (citing Aguinis 2018/2023 transparency work + Hickman 2022 + Tay 2022) rather than hedge about coding-vocabulary thinness.
- §7 can claim forward-looking agenda-setting (citing Lazer et al. 2020 + Bail 2024 + Edelmann 2020) because the paper is now in conversation with the CSS and AI-in-research literatures.
- Appendix E / F can be re-framed as instrument-successor-to (Aguinis 2023, Solarino 2019, Gwet 2014) rather than as standalone artifacts.

**Dimension 3 closure — argumentative structure:**
The argumentation gap was: "gap and solution deferred to §3; counterarguments deferred to §6." Reference expansion allows:
- §1 to *name* the gap by citing what prior work has and has not done: *"Prior reviews of AI in social science (Ziems et al. 2024; Bail 2024; Luo et al. 2024) catalogue capabilities; ORM audits (Aguinis et al. 2018, 2023; Hickman et al. 2022) build role-level rubrics for published research; no existing instrument adjudicates automation claims at the role × case level. This paper fills that gap."*
- §6 to integrate rival-explanation treatment with forward-looking citation (Gelman & Loken 2014; Open Science Collaboration 2015; Nosek et al. 2018) rather than reading as retroactive apology.
- §7 to close with "next research directions" citations (Lazer et al. 2020; Edelmann et al. 2020) in the template mode, rather than the chiasmus close.
