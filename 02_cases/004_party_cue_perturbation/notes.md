# Case 4: Party-cue perturbation in political-text annotation

## Summary

Hold policy text constant while varying party cues to test whether AI coding tracks politically loaded labels rather than substantive content.

## Why it matters for typology

It makes model-mediated political bias visible at the measurement stage.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Cue changes do not systematically move outputs
- Main confounds: Party labels can sometimes be substantively relevant context rather than pure bias
- Evidence status: Peer-reviewed article

## Sources collected

- `C004_P1` (peer-reviewed article / open): LLMs as annotators: the effect of party cues on labelling decisions by large language models — https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05834-4
