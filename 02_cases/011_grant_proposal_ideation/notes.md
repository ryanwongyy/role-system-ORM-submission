# Case 11: LLM-assisted grant proposal ideation in competitive funding calls

## Summary

Compare AI-assisted and non-assisted proposal ideation under real funding calls to test whether AI changes what gets proposed, not just how it is written.

## Why it matters for typology

Without a funding case, relevance adjudication remains underspecified and too abstract.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: AI use leaves distinctiveness and stakeholder relevance unchanged or improves both
- Main confounds: Austerity, field maturity, and call design can compress agendas even without AI
- Evidence status: Preprint

## Sources collected

- `C011_P1` (preprint / open): The Rise of Large Language Models and the Direction and Impact of US Federal Research Funding — https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15485
- `C011_S1` (preprint / open): Can LLMs Generate Novel Research Ideas? A Large-Scale Human Study with 100+ NLP Researchers — https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.04109
