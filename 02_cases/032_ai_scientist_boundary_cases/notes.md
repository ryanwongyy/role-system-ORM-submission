# Case 32: The AI Scientist / AI Scientist-v2 as end-to-end automation boundary cases

## Summary

Use fully automated scientific-discovery systems as boundary cases to see which role claims are social-science-specific and which generalize across autonomous research.

## Why it matters for typology

Useful only as a boundary case; it should not be mistaken for direct evidence about social science.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Autonomous systems generalize cleanly to social-science workflows without role-specific adjustments
- Main confounds: Most evidence comes from AI/ML research rather than social science
- Evidence status: Peer-reviewed / preprint mix

## Sources collected

- `C032_P1` (preprint / open): The AI Scientist: Towards Fully Automated Open-Ended Scientific Discovery — https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.06292
- `C032_S1` (preprint / open): The AI Scientist-v2: Workshop-Level Automated Scientific Discovery via Agentic Tree Search — https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066
