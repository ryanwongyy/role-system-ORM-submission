# Case 31: Project Rachel and AI scholarly identity as an authorship boundary case

## Summary

Study how publishers, reviewers, and citation networks respond when AI is packaged as a named scholarly author.

## Why it matters for typology

It pressures the legitimacy and authorship edges of the typology more than routine co-writing cases do.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: The scholarly ecosystem treats AI authorship no differently from AI-assisted human authorship
- Main confounds: Project Rachel is provocative and small-scale; it may overstate institutional reaction
- Evidence status: Preprint / public project
