# Case 34: AgentSociety and generative social simulation as a transportability boundary case

## Summary

Use large-scale LLM social simulation to test whether calibration and transportability become separate human roles when simulated societies are used for policy insight.

## Why it matters for typology

It is a strong pressure test for predictive-role splitting beyond surveys and experiments.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Simulation outputs transport reliably enough that calibration and transportability collapse into one role
- Main confounds: Agent-based simulations may look realistic while still missing institutional mechanisms
- Evidence status: Preprint
