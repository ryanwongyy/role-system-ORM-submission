# Case 18: QDA delegation preferences: human-only versus human-initiated versus AI-initiated coding

## Summary

Study how qualitative researchers choose among delegation levels in coding and thematic analysis.

## Why it matters for typology

It is one of the best cases for role splitting inside qualitative analysis without requiring live fieldwork.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Researchers show no meaningful distinction between delegation levels or between ownership and context concerns
- Main confounds: Self-reported preferences may not match real delegated practice
- Evidence status: Preprint

## Sources collected

- `C018_P1` (preprint / open): Not a Collaborator or a Supervisor, but an Assistant: Striking the Balance Between Efficiency and Ownership in AI-incorporated Qualitative Data Analysis — https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.18297
- `C018_S1` (preprint / open): From Assistance to Autonomy -- A Researcher Study on the Potential of AI Support for Qualitative Data Analysis — https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.19275
