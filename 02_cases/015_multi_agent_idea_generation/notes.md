# Case 15: Multi-agent scientific idea generation as a boundary case for social-science agenda work

## Summary

Treat agent teams that brainstorm, critique, and refine ideas as a boundary case for whether collaborative AI can displace human agenda setting.

## Why it matters for typology

Helpful because it pressures the temptation to generalize from scientific ideation benchmarks to social-science role claims.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Multi-agent idea generation produces socially relevant and mechanism-rich agendas without human filtering
- Main confounds: Most evidence comes from non-social-science domains and model-internal evaluations
- Evidence status: Preprint

## Sources collected

- `C015_P1` (preprint / open): Many Heads Are Better Than One: Improved Scientific Idea Generation by A LLM-Based Multi-Agent System — https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.09403
- `C015_S1` (preprint / open): Can LLMs Generate Novel Research Ideas? A Large-Scale Human Study with 100+ NLP Researchers — https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.04109
