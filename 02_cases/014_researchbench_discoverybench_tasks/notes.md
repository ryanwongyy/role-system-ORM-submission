# Case 14: ResearchBench / DiscoveryBench sociology and economics discovery tasks

## Summary

Use benchmark tasks drawn from sociology and economics to test whether AI supports inspiration retrieval, hypothesis composition, or ranking differently.

## Why it matters for typology

It is one of the few ways to decompose early-stage discovery work with controlled tasks.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: LLMs perform equally well across retrieval, composition, and ranking on social-science tasks
- Main confounds: Benchmarks simplify open-ended theory work and may understate institutional context
- Evidence status: Preprint / benchmark

## Sources collected

- `C014_P1` (benchmark / open): ResearchBench: Benchmarking LLMs in Scientific Discovery via Inspiration-Based Task Decomposition — https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.21248
- `C014_S1` (benchmark / open): DiscoveryBench: Towards Data-Driven Discovery with Large Language Models — https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.01725
