# Case 12: Funding-pipeline agenda compression under LLM-assisted proposal writing

## Summary

Link inferred AI involvement in submitted proposals to semantic distinctiveness, reviewer reactions, and downstream publication novelty.

## Why it matters for typology

It converts abstract worries about agenda compression into observable portfolio-level evidence.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Higher AI involvement predicts equal or greater long-run novelty
- Main confounds: Causal inference is hard because ambitious labs may both use AI and write better proposals
- Evidence status: Preprint

## Sources collected

- `C012_P1` (preprint / open): The Rise of Large Language Models and the Direction and Impact of US Federal Research Funding — https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15485
