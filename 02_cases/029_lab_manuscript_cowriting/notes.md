# Case 29: Lab manuscript co-writing with version histories and authorship disclosure

## Summary

Track how AI-assisted drafting redistributes visible and invisible labor across PIs, doctoral students, and lab managers.

## Why it matters for typology

It makes hidden labor and ownership visible instead of assuming them.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: AI drafting neither redistributes credit nor changes evaluation after quality is controlled
- Main confounds: Pre-existing hierarchy may explain much of the credit allocation
- Evidence status: Science paper / mixed evidence

## Sources collected

- `C029_P1` (preprint / open): Scientific production in the era of Large Language Models — https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13187
- `C029_S1` (preprint / open): Reactive Writers: How Co-Writing with AI Changes How We Engage with Ideas — https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10374
