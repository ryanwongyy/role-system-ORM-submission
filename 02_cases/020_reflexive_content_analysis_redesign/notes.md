# Case 20: LLM-guided reflexive content analysis and codebook redesign

## Summary

Use reflexive content analysis to test whether AI support works only when human researchers redesign prompts, codebooks, and interpretive checkpoints.

## Why it matters for typology

Useful for seeing whether qualitative rigor comes from the workflow redesign rather than from the model alone.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Simple one-shot prompting performs as well as redesigned reflexive workflows
- Main confounds: Method-specific guidance may overfit a narrow coding style
- Evidence status: Peer-reviewed article

## Sources collected

- `C020_P1` (peer-reviewed article / open): Scaling hermeneutics: a guide to qualitative coding with LLMs for reflexive content analysis — https://link.springer.com/article/10.1140/epjds/s13688-025-00548-8
