# Case 9: Citation-grounded multi-paper synthesis for social-policy literatures

## Summary

Use retrieval-augmented synthesis to generate evidence maps or literature-survey memos from a fixed social-policy corpus with citation checking.

## Why it matters for typology

It pressures provenance architecture and accountability rather than simple summarization.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Grounded and non-grounded systems perform equally well on claim support and coverage
- Main confounds: OpenScholar is not social-science-specific, so transfer to policy literatures must be verified
- Evidence status: Peer-reviewed article
