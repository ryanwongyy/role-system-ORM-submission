# Case 3: Multi-perspective annotation of political incivility or offensiveness

## Summary

Label the same texts by demographic or partisan subgroups and compare majority-vote, AI-only, and targeted human-sampling corrections.

## Why it matters for typology

It can force a split in the starting typology rather than merely confirm it.

## Caveats

- Falsification test: Majority-vote or AI-only labels preserve subgroup-specific inferences equally well
- Main confounds: Task underspecification may masquerade as genuine perspectival disagreement
- Evidence status: Preprint

## Sources collected

- `C003_P1` (preprint / open): Multi-Perspective LLM Annotations for Valid Analyses in Subjective Tasks — https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21404
